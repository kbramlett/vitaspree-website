Benefits of Red Bell Peppers

written & posted by KaDee Bramlett
Red Foods

They're juicy, crunchy, sweet, & packed with anti-inflammatory & antioxidant components

Let's take a moment to appreciate the red bell pepper. They're easy to find in grocery stores and farmer's markets, they're gorgeous, and they pack an excellent nutritional punch.

Nutrient Profile and Health Benefits

At approximately 40 calories per cup, chopped, red bell peppers are a low-calorie and tasty way to add colorful drama to your meals while incorporating something wholesome that promotes better health.

This non-spicy pepper contains many minerals, vitamins, and other nutrients that promote healthy eyes, skin, immune function, and so much more.

Carotenoids are the pigments in red bell peppers that give them their vibrant color. Your body converts these antioxidants and anti-inflammatory carotenoids into Vitamin A, which is essential for healthy eyes, arteries, and immune function.

Red bell peppers are also a good source of B Vitamins and a great source of the powerful antioxidant Vitamin C at approximately 200% of your Daily Value in a one-cup serving.

But wait! There's more! Red bell peppers contain important minerals, like potassium and manganese, which are needed for proper cellular function, muscle contraction, and healthy bones and connective tissues.

How to Prepare

Important Note--For optimum nutrient absorption, pair red bell pepper with a healthy source of fat since its important vitamins are fat soluble.

Red bell peppers are delicious raw, as a garnish, sautéed, roasted...It's hard to go wrong!

  • Raw: Raw slices of this delicately flavored pepper make a super satisfying, crunchy snack along with a handful of seeds or nuts, a healthy, whole-food fat source.
  • Dipped: Dip red bell pepper spears in hummus or other complementary dip (made with minimally processed olive oil).
  • Garnish: Dice them up and throw them on top of a salad for an excellent nutrient boost and a beautiful pop of color.
  • Sautéed: Slice or dice them and throw them in a skillet with some chopped onion for a quick veggie fajita blend and top a bowl of beans and rice with them.
  • Baked: Cut off the top of the bell pepper, and remove the seeds and ribs. Stuff them with a blend of beans and seasoned quinoa or rice. Bake until they begin to soften.

How to select and store

Choose red bell peppers with firm flesh and the most vibrant color.

Make sure they are dry and store them in the refrigerator in a drawer.

Note: Don’t feel pressured to eat the whole pepper in one sitting if you don’t want to! You can cut off the portion you want and store the rest in a sealed container for a day or two until you're ready to use the rest of it.

Mindful eating prompt

  • Notice the vibrant, red color of the pepper.
  • Admire the structure of this beautiful and nutritious food.
  • Take a moment to enjoy the fresh fragrance before you take a bite.
  • As you chew the pepper, direct your awareness to the crunchy texture and the juiciness that is released in your mouth as your teeth break through the cell walls.
  • Is there a delicious synergy between the red bell pepper and other ingredients in the dish you’ve prepared? Take note of the complementary flavors.
  • Savor this beautiful food with all your senses, and enjoy the moment of gratitude.

Conclusion

Red bell peppers are a versatile and delicious choice for adding more nutrients, fiber, and color to any meal or snack. Remember to choose the brightest red peppers available and pair them with a healthy fat for the most effective absorption of nutrients.

Happy & healthy eating!
