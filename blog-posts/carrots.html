The Carrot

written & posted by KaDee Bramlett
Orange Foods

With a satisfying crunch and a host beneficial nutrients, carrots are a delicious addition to any diet.

Hungry for a snack? Reach for a carrot! These colorful, crunchy, sweet root veggies are packed with beneficial nutrients that promote better immune function, cardiovascular health, strong bones, better digestion, healthier skin and eyes, and the list goes on!

Nutrient Profile and Health Benefits

Carrots contain Vitamins C and K, antioxidant properties, potassium, fiber, and beta-carotene (which your body converts into Vitamin A).

Vitamin C and beta-carotene are antioxidants, which protect your body from cellular damage and boost your immune system. Vitamin K is vital for bone health and proper blood clotting.

Fiber, which is found in carrots as well as virtually all plant-based foods, is one of the best anti-inflammatory agents you can consume. Fiber is a prebiotic that feeds our good gut bacteria. The recommended daily amount of fiber is 25 grams for women and 38 grams for men.

When our good bacteria are well-fed with fiber, they signal to our immune system that things are in balance by producing a short-chain fatty acid called butyrate. The anti-inflammatory signal is sent throughout the body as butyrate travels through the bloodstream.

Raw or Cooked? The nutrient profile of carrots differs a bit whether they’re raw or cooked. Try to mix it up by eating them in a variety of preparations: raw, roasted, boiled, etc. At only 50 calories per cup, you'll be able to experiment with carrots in lots of dishes and not worry about the caloric intake.

How to Prepare

Note: Some of the nutrients in carrots are fat-soluble, so pair them with healthy, whole-food sources of fat for optimum nutrient absorption. Examples of whole-food sources of fat include nuts, seeds, and avocados.

Need some inspiration for how to incorporate carrots into your diet? Keep reading!

  • Raw: It doesn’t get easier than grabbing a carrot, giving it a quick scrub, and then boom, you have a satisfying snack! Bugs Bunny knows what’s up!
  • Dipped: Dipping carrot spears into nut butter or hummus (made with a minimally processed oil) is an excellent way to pair them with healthy fats for better nutrient absorption.
  • Steamed: Are you working on replacing meat products in your diet with more plant-based ones? Try this fun plant-based hotdog recipe from the Whole Food Plant Based Cooking Show!
  • Boiled: Carrots make a tasty and colorful addition to soups or stews. Chop them into whatever size and shape you prefer and toss them into the pot with other whole-food plant-based ingredients like potatoes, celery, cabbage, or any other vegetables you enjoy!
  • Roasted: Looking for a delicious, nutritious side dish? Check out this oil-free roasted veggie recipe by Minimalist Baker

Fun fact: Did you know you can eat the green tops of carrots?! You can eat the leaves and the stems! They taste a lot like parsley and can be added to salads, soups, wraps...whatever you want, really! Follow your palate!

Other Suggestions: There’s no need to peel carrots when eating them raw or cooked. Just be sure to wash them thoroughly before eating them.

To pair a healthy fat with oil-free roasted veggies, garnish carrots with chopped walnuts. I’m learning that so many dishes are amazing with a sprinkle of chopped walnuts! I hope you love it, too!

How to Select and Store

Choose the brightest orange carrots you can find. More pigment means more nutrients!

Make sure the carrots are firm. If they’re past their prime, they’ll be limp, dry, and rubbery.

Inspect the green tops, if present. They should look fresh, crisp, and have a vibrant green hue.

If you intend to eat the carrots raw and whole, choose ones that are relatively slender for easier bites.

Store them dry in the vegetable drawer of the refrigerator with the green tops removed if you plan to store them for an extended period. No need to store them in a sealed plastic bag or container.

Mindful Eating Prompt

  • Notice the texture of the carrot’s surface—the horizontal lines and dimples that formed as the carrot grew.
  • Admire the leaf structure of the carrot’s top. These complex and delicate fronds that powered the formation of the carrot and now hold nutrients that will nourish your body.
  • Relish the grassy, vegetal fragrance of the carrot.
  • Is the carrot raw or cooked? If it’s raw, enjoy the snap that occurs when you bite into it. If it’s cooked, appreciate the comforting and soft quality as you bite into it.
  • Is the carrot sweet and juicy or is it more earthy in flavor? Have you paired the carrot with something else? Savor the blending of flavors you’ve created.
  • Let this moment of gratitude nourish your soul as you nourish your body.

Conclusion

Carrots are full of beneficial vitamins, minerals, and fiber. They make a great snack and are versatile enough to add to a wide variety of dishes. In addition, they’re fun to eat and with a little awareness during meal or snack time, they provide a feast for all the senses! Hip-hip-hooray for carrots!
