What you'll learn

What is a whole-plant-based food? What's the difference between a processed food and a whole food? Why does it matter?

What is "processing" when it comes to food? In reality, processing is not always bad. Some foods need certain types of processing to enhance their nutrient profiles inside our bodies. There are other foods that we need to eat raw and/or cooked to get their full benefits.

Sometimes the answers to these questions are simple, and sometimes it takes a little more knowledge to make the best decisions for you.

How to prepare your environment for success

I know it's obvious, but preparation is key to success. I'll help you devise strategies to prepare your environment in a way that fits your lifestyle and needs. Along the way, we'll tweak the plan to optimize for you over the long term.

With practice and patience, one day you'll realize that incorporating whole-plant-based foods into your diet takes very little thought and effort. Get ready to cultivate excellent habits that feel easy and make you feel great!

How to navigate the grocery store in search of whole-plant-based foods

We'll talk about efficient ways to read food labels and learn to identify common ingredients that are actually just processed sugar, salt, and "bad" fat in disguise.

You'll also learn which areas of the grocery store to avoid, and we'll cover some easy-to-find highly nutritious options that are often overlooked or dismissed.

How to replace refined sugars, excess salt, and unhealthy fats

It's common knowledge that too much sugar, salt, and fat are not ideal for optimum health.

We'll talk about how to incorporate healthful alternatives to these kinds of ingredients. Many of these alternatives are not just "less bad," actually, they're often health-promoting! Wouldn't it be great to learn how to make food that tastes amazing without the guilt of eating too much sugar, salt, and fat?!

We'll cover these topics and more!

Vibrantly pigmented foods come packed with vital nutrients

Quick Tip: Let color guide your choices. Colorful foods signal the presence of vitamins, minerals, antioxidants, fiber, and more! These essential nutrients lead to greater satiety, promote immune function, brain health, and gut health. Brightly colored whole foods also contain anti-aging and anti-inflammatory properties and help curb spikes in blood sugar, too.
